Learn the Basics of Web3

New to crypto? Here's what you need to know:

1. Introduction

A blockchain is a digital ledger or record book that is public, transparent, and unchangeable. Instead of being stored on one computer, it’s shared across many, making it decentralized. It powers cryptocurrencies, NFTs, and other Web3 technologies.

2. What is Cryptocurrency?

A cryptocurrency is digital money that uses blockchain technology to secure and verify transactions. Examples include Bitcoin and Ethereum. On Luck Wallet, we use a fake cryptocurrency called $LUCK for learning and gaming purposes only.

3. What are NFTs?

NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens) are unique digital items (like art, avatars, cards) that you can own and trade. Each one is one-of-a-kind, and its ownership is stored on the blockchain. On Luck Wallet, all NFTs are simulated, so you can practice minting, buying, and selling them.

4. What is a Wallet?

A crypto wallet is a tool used to store and manage your cryptocurrencies and NFTs. It has a public address (like your username) and a private key (like your password). In Luck Wallet, each user gets a fake wallet address to simulate real usage.

5. What is a Web3 App?

A Web3 app is a decentralized application that interacts with the blockchain. Instead of using traditional login systems, Web3 apps rely on wallet addresses. Luck Wallet mimics this setup in a safe, centralized way for learning.
